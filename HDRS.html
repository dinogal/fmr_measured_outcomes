Hamilton Depression Rating Scale (HAM-D)

Score each item according to the patient's symptoms. Only the first 17 items count toward the total score.

Depressed Mood – sadness, hopeless, helpless, worthless.
Feelings of Guilt – self-reproach, feels punished, delusions of guilt.
Suicide – wishes he/she were dead, thoughts of death, suicide attempts.
Insomnia (Early) – difficulty falling asleep.
Insomnia (Middle) – patient complains of being restless and disturbed during the night.
Insomnia (Late) – waking in early hours of the morning and unable to fall asleep again.
Work and Activities – loss of interest, decreased activity, inability to work, hobbies dropped.
Psychomotor Retardation – slowed speech, decreased motor activity.
Agitation – restless, fidgety, unable to sit still.
Anxiety (Psychic) – worry, irritability, fears, panic attacks.
Anxiety (Somatic) – physical symptoms related to anxiety.
Somatic Symptoms (Gastrointestinal) – loss of appetite, heavy feelings in abdomen, constipation.
Somatic Symptoms (General) – heaviness in limbs, backache, loss of energy, fatigue.
Genital Symptoms – loss of libido, menstrual disturbances.
Hypochondriasis – preoccupation with ill health.
Loss of Weight – according to patient or measured.
Insight – acknowledges being depressed and ill.
Diurnal Variation (Not scored toward total) – mood worse in morning or evening.
Depersonalization/Derealization (Not scored toward total) – feelings of unreality.
Paranoid Symptoms (Not scored toward total) – suspiciousness, delusions of persecution.
Obsessive and Compulsive Symptoms (Not scored toward total) – repetitive thoughts or behaviors.
